Privacy & Data Protection

Ekra Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Ekra (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects your information when you use the Ekra platform, including our website, mobile applications, and related services for listing, discovering, and booking spaces and experiences.

Status Effective
Scope Visitors, users, hosts, and guests of Ekra

1. Who this policy applies to

This Privacy Policy applies to:

  • Individuals who browse our website or apps;
  • Users who create an Ekra account (hosts and guests);
  • Individuals who communicate with us, for example via contact forms or support;
  • Any person whose information we process in connection with the platform.

By using Ekra, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, you should not use the platform.

2. Information we collect

We collect information in the following categories:

  • Account & contact information: name, email address, phone number, password hash, and profile details you choose to provide (such as profile photo, biography, language preferences).
  • Listing & booking information: details about listings you create (title, description, photos, availability, pricing, location details as provided by you) and information about bookings and reservations (dates, number of guests, messages between hosts and guests, booking status).
  • Payment & transaction information: limited payment-related data in connection with charges and payouts, such as transaction identifiers, amounts, currency, and status. Sensitive card or banking details are processed by our payment providers (for example, Moyasar or other payment gateways) and are not stored in plain form by Ekra.
  • Communications & support: content of messages you send via our messaging features, contact forms, reviews, feedback, and any support correspondence.
  • Technical & usage data: log data, IP address, device identifiers, browser type, operating system, access times, and pages viewed, which help us secure the platform and improve performance.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: information collected through cookies, pixels, and similar tools to remember your preferences, keep you signed in, and analyze how the service is used.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To create, maintain, and secure your Ekra account;
  • To enable you to list, discover, and book spaces or experiences;
  • To process and manage payments, refunds, and payouts through our payment partners;
  • To send transactional communications, such as booking confirmations, updates, and support messages;
  • To personalize your experience, including recommended listings or content;
  • To monitor and improve the safety, security, and performance of the platform;
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service;
  • To respond to your inquiries, requests, or complaints.

4. Legal bases for processing

Where applicable data protection laws require a legal basis (for example, under the GDPR), we rely on one or more of the following:

  • Performance of a contract – to provide the Ekra service you request, including managing your account, listings, and bookings.
  • Legitimate interests – to secure our platform, prevent fraud or abuse, improve our services, and communicate with you about features you use (balanced against your rights and interests).
  • Consent – for certain cookies, marketing communications, or optional data you choose to provide. You can withdraw consent at any time where applicable.
  • Legal obligations – to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and lawful requests from authorities.

5. How we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information as follows:

  • Between users: certain information is shared between hosts and guests when necessary for a booking or communication (for example, name, profile details, listing information, and booking details).
  • Service providers: with trusted third parties who perform services on our behalf, such as payment processing (e.g., Moyasar or other gateways), cloud hosting and storage, email delivery, analytics, or customer support. These providers are bound by contractual confidentiality and data protection obligations.
  • Compliance and protection: with law enforcement, regulators, or other parties when we believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect the rights, property, or safety of Ekra, our users, or others, or to detect, prevent, or address fraud, security, or technical issues.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, restructuring, sale of assets, or similar corporate transaction, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to continued protection consistent with this policy.

6. International transfers

Depending on your location and on where our infrastructure and service providers operate, your information may be transferred to and processed in countries that may have different data protection laws than your country of residence.

Where required by law, we take appropriate safeguards to protect your information in connection with such transfers, such as using standard contractual clauses or equivalent protections approved by competent authorities.

7. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, including:

  • Providing the services you request and maintaining your account;
  • Maintaining a record of transactions, bookings, and communications as required for accounting, dispute resolution, or legal obligations;
  • Complying with applicable laws and regulations.

When information is no longer needed, we will delete it or anonymize it, unless we are required by law to retain it for a longer period.

8. Your rights and choices

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Request deletion of your personal information, where allowed by law;
  • Object to or request restriction of certain processing activities;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on your consent;
  • Request a copy of your data in a portable format, where technically feasible.

You can exercise many of these rights directly through your account settings. For additional requests, please contact us using the details provided in the “How to contact us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

9. Cookies and similar technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate and improve Ekra, including to:

  • Keep you signed in and remember your preferences;
  • Secure your session and protect against fraud;
  • Measure and analyze how the platform is used.

Depending on your location, you may see a cookie notice or banner when you first visit Ekra. You can typically control cookies through your browser settings. If you disable certain cookies, some features of the platform may not function properly.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. This includes industry-standard security practices at the application and infrastructure level.

However, no online service can be completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your account credentials confidential and for notifying us promptly if you suspect any unauthorized access to your account.

11. Children’s privacy

Ekra is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children without appropriate consent as required by law. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information without such consent, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete the information.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, for example to reflect changes to our services or legal requirements. When we make material changes, we will take reasonable steps to notify you, such as by posting an updated policy on this page and updating the “effective” date.

Your continued use of Ekra after the updated policy becomes effective means that you accept the changes.

13. How to contact us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our handling of your personal information, you can contact us using the support or contact options provided within the Ekra platform, or by using the contact details published on our official website.

When you contact us, we may request additional information (for example, proof of identity) if reasonably necessary to verify your request and protect your account and data against unauthorized access.

14. Region-specific information

Because Ekra may be used by individuals in different jurisdictions, this section provides additional information for certain regions. Where there is any conflict between this section and the general terms of the Privacy Policy, the more protective rule for the individual will apply, to the extent required by applicable law.

14.1 European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, and similar jurisdictions

If you are located in a country that applies the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, or a similar comprehensive data protection law, Ekra will generally act as a “data controller” for personal data we process about you in connection with operating the platform. Our legal bases for processing are described in Section 4 above.

In addition to the rights described in Section 8, you may also have the right to request that we restrict certain processing, to object to processing that is based on our legitimate interests, and to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. You can find contact details for EU data protection authorities via the European Data Protection Board (EDPB), and for the UK via the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

14.2 California and certain U.S. states

If you are a resident of California or another U.S. state with similar privacy laws (for example, the CCPA/CPRA in California), you may have specific rights to know, access, correct, and delete certain personal information we hold about you, as well as rights related to the “sale” or “sharing” of your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Ekra does not sell personal information for monetary compensation. To the extent our use of analytics or advertising cookies could be interpreted as “sharing” under applicable law, you may have the right to opt out of such sharing. Where such tools are implemented, we will provide mechanisms (for example, cookie banners or account settings) that allow you to manage your preferences.

You will not be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights, but certain features may be unavailable if we are unable to process your information (for example, where deletion of your data means we can no longer provide your account or bookings history).

15. Data hosting, infrastructure, and logs

Ekra is built on modern cloud infrastructure. Our production environment is hosted with reputable cloud service providers, which may include Amazon Web Services (AWS) and other infrastructure platforms. These providers operate secure data centers with industry-recognized certifications and safeguards, and they process data only according to our documented instructions.

Application and server logs may include technical metadata such as IP addresses, browser or device identifiers, request timestamps, error traces, and other diagnostic information. We use these logs to monitor the reliability, security, and performance of Ekra, to detect and prevent abuse, and to troubleshoot issues. Log data is retained only for as long as necessary for these operational purposes and is subject to access controls.

Where we use AWS or other cloud providers to store data (including backups), we rely on the security measures offered by those providers such as encryption at rest, encryption in transit, network segmentation, access logging, and multi-factor authentication for administrative access. We also implement our own application-level safeguards, including authentication and authorization controls for Ekra users and internal staff.

16. Third-party services and integrations

In addition to cloud hosting, Ekra may integrate with several third-party services that act as independent controllers or processors of personal data. These may include, for example, payment gateways, email delivery platforms, analytics providers, identity or fraud-prevention services, communication tools, and customer support platforms.

Where these third parties act as our processors, we enter into agreements that require them to protect personal data, use it only for the purposes we specify, and apply appropriate security measures. Where they act as independent controllers (for example, when you create a separate account with a payment provider), their own privacy policies will govern their use of your data. We encourage you to review those policies carefully.

We may update the list of third-party services we use from time to time as we improve Ekra. Where required by law, we will provide additional notice or obtain your consent before enabling new categories of processing that are materially different from what is described in this Privacy Policy.

17. Automated decision-making and profiling

Ekra may use limited forms of automated processing to improve your experience on the platform. Examples include ranking or recommending listings based on factors such as availability, category, location, past searches, or general popularity, or highlighting hosts and listings that have higher ratings or better response times.

These types of automated processing are intended to organize and personalize content for you and do not involve solely automated decisions that produce legal effects or similarly significant impacts on you within the meaning of certain data protection laws. If we ever introduce forms of automated decision-making that have such significant effects, we will do so in accordance with applicable legal requirements and will provide you with additional information about the logic involved and your rights in relation to such processing.

This Privacy Policy is provided for general informational purposes regarding how Ekra handles personal data in connection with the platform. It is not intended to create or replace legal rights or obligations beyond those required under applicable law. You should review this page periodically for the most current version, as we may update it to reflect new features, regulatory guidance, or industry best practices.
